# What determines different tax factors
Here’s a **brief mapping of which boxes on Form W‑2 and Form 1098 contribute to each calculation:**

### • Net income calculation

(Income minus deductions)

* **W-2 Box 1 – Wages, tips, other compensation:** starting point for taxable earned income.
* **W-2 Box 12 – Pre-tax deductions (e.g., 401(k), HSA codes):** already excluded from Box 1 and affect net taxable income.
* **1098 Box 1 – Mortgage interest received:** deductible itemized deduction that reduces taxable income.
* **1098 Box 5 – Mortgage insurance premiums (if applicable):** may be deductible and reduce taxable income.

---

### • Tax bracket determination

(Based on taxable income level)

* **W-2 Box 1 – Wages:** primary income used to determine taxable income range.
* **1098 Box 1 – Mortgage interest:** lowers taxable income if itemized, potentially moving you into a lower bracket.

---

### • Predictive tax refund estimation

(Compare taxes owed vs. taxes already paid)

* **W-2 Box 2 – Federal income tax withheld:** main amount compared to calculated tax liability.
* **W-2 Box 17 – State income tax withheld:** used for state refund/amount owed estimates.
* **W-2 Box 4 & Box 6 – Social Security and Medicare withheld:** relevant for payroll tax reconciliation but typically not refund calculations.
* **1098 Box 1 – Mortgage interest:** affects refund by reducing taxable income and thus tax owed.

---

### • Income classification (taxable vs. non-taxable)

* **W-2 Box 1 vs. Boxes 3 & 5:** shows what wages are taxable for federal income tax vs. Social Security/Medicare.
* **W-2 Box 12 codes:** identify pre-tax benefits (e.g., retirement contributions, HSAs) that reduce taxable income.
* **1098 boxes:** generally represent **deductible expenses**, not income, helping classify amounts that reduce taxable income rather than add to it.

---

✅ **In short:**

* **W-2 Box 1 and Box 2** drive most income and refund calculations.
* **W-2 Box 12** explains adjustments to taxable income.
* **1098 Box 1** mainly impacts **deductions**, lowering taxable income rather than adding income.
